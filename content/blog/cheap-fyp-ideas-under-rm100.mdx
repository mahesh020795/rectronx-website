## How to Make a Cheap Project Still Score Well

A low-cost build doesn't have to mean a low mark. The things that actually move your grade:

- **Document your design decisions.** Explain why you picked a cheaper sensor over a pricier one — this shows deliberate engineering judgement, not just budget constraints.
- **Add a software layer even on simple hardware.** A basic data log to an SD card or a simple mobile app turns a RM30 build into a project with real technical depth.
- **Test it properly and show the data.** A cheap sensor that's been calibrated and tested over a week beats an expensive one that's only been switched on for the demo.
- **Be upfront about limitations.** Panels respect students who understand what their budget build can't do, rather than overselling it.

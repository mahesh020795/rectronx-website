One question every student asks before starting their project is simple: how much will this cost? The honest answer is that **FYP cost** in Malaysia ranges from almost nothing to over a thousand ringgit, depending on what you build. In this guide we break down the real **FYP cost** with actual component prices in RM, cover the typical **Arduino price Malaysia** students pay, compare cheap versus expensive parts, and share practical ways to save money.

## What Drives FYP Cost?

Your **FYP cost** depends mostly on three things:

- **Hardware vs software:** pure software projects can cost RM0; hardware projects always cost more.
- **Sensors and modules:** the more sensors, the higher the bill.
- **Mechanical parts:** motors, chassis, and 3D-printed parts add up fast.

A web app FYP might cost nothing but your time. A robotics project can easily pass RM600.

## Cheap vs Expensive Parts: When to Spend

Not every component deserves the same budget. Here is where to save and where to spend:

**Save on (cheap is fine):**
- Jumper wires, breadboards, resistors, LEDs
- Common sensors like HC-SR04 and DHT11
- Clone microcontroller boards for prototyping

**Spend a bit more on:**
- Power supplies — a bad one causes random resets and wasted debugging hours
- Motor drivers and motors — cheap ones overheat or fail
- Anything safety-related (lithium batteries, chargers)
- Sensors where accuracy matters (use DHT22 over DHT11, genuine current sensors)

The false economy is buying the cheapest power supply or motor driver, then losing a week to mysterious bugs.

## Smart Ways to Reduce FYP Cost

You can cut your **FYP cost** significantly with a few habits:

1. **Buy a starter kit** instead of individual parts — kits bundle a board, sensors, and wires cheaper than buying separately.
2. **Share bulk orders** with coursemates to split shipping and hit price tiers.
3. **Reuse parts** from earlier lab work or seniors who have finished their FYP.
4. **Borrow expensive tools** (multimeter, soldering station) instead of buying.
5. **Order early from local Penang shops** for cheap small parts to avoid rush shipping fees.
6. **Prototype with clones**, and only buy genuine parts if reliability becomes an issue.

## Hidden Costs Students Forget

Beyond the main components, budget a little extra for:

- Spare sensors and wires (cheap parts fail)
- 3D printing or enclosure costs
- Printing and binding the thesis
- Poster printing for the FYP showcase

These small items can quietly add RM50 to RM150 to your total **FYP cost**.
